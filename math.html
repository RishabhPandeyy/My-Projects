Mathematics-I (BAS103) Syllabus - AKTU

Unit 1: Matrices

  • Types of matrices: Symmetric, Skew-symmetric, Orthogonal, Complex, Unitary
  • Inverse and rank of a matrix using elementary transformations
  • Rank-Nullity theorem
  • System of linear equations
  • Characteristic equation
  • Cayley-Hamilton Theorem and its applications
  • Diagonalization of a matrix

Unit 2: Differential Calculus – I

  • Limits, continuity, and differentiability
  • Rolle’s Theorem
  • Lagrange’s and Cauchy’s Mean Value Theorems
  • Successive differentiation and nth order derivatives
  • Leibnitz’s theorem and its applications
  • Envelope, involutes, and evolutes
  • Curve tracing in Cartesian and polar coordinates

Unit 3: Differential Calculus – II

  • Partial derivatives and total derivatives
  • Euler’s theorem for homogeneous functions
  • Taylor and Maclaurin’s theorems for functions of one and two variables
  • Maxima and minima of functions of several variables
  • Lagrange’s method of multipliers
  • Jacobians

Unit 4: Multivariable Calculus – I

  • Double integrals
  • Triple integrals
  • Applications: Areas, volumes, center of mass, and center of gravity (with constant and variable densities)

Unit 5: Vector Calculus

  • Vector identities (without proof)
  • Vector differentiation: Gradient, curl, divergence, and their physical interpretations
  • Directional derivatives
  • Vector integration: Line, surface, and volume integrals
  • Gauss’s Divergence Theorem (without proof) and applications
  • Green’s Theorem (without proof) and applications
  • Stokes’ Theorem (without proof) and applications

Note: This syllabus is based on the AKTU curriculum. Refer to official sources for the most current version.
